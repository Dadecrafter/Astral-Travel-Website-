The Sun!

Facts and Info

General Information:

The Sun, which is roughly 91 Million Miles from the Earth, is a Yellow Dwarf Star at the center of the Solar System. Its mass is 330,000 times that of Earth and is made up of about 73% Hydrogen and about 25% Helium. The Sun can aslo reach temperatures of about 5,780 Kelvin.


How Does It Make Energy?

In order the make the energy, the Sun undergos a process called "Nuclear Fission." The high pressure and temperature in the Sun allows it to seperate Nuclei from their Electrons and thus Hydrogen nuclei then fuse into Helium atoms which releases an enormous amount of energy.

What Does It Do For The Earth?

The Sun is the most important energy source for the Earth, it provides the energy needed for plants to undergo photosynthesis and thereby make energy and grow, and as you know animals need plants to eat. And also without the heat of the Sun, the Earth would freeze over.

The Sun's Lifecycle.
